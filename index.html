About Me

I am a theoretical physics PhD student working at the intersection of quantum information theory and condensed matter physics. I am co-supervised by Dr. Robert Raussendorf at Leibniz University of Hannover, Germany, and Dr. Mona Berciu at the University of British Columbia, Canada. My research primarily focuses on the topic of computational phases of matter, through which we aim to tackle the question: "What is the source of quantum advantage?"

Research

My research explores advanced topics in condensed matter physics and quantum computing. I work on understanding computational phases of matter and seek to uncover the fundamental sources of quantum advantage.

Projects

Here are some of the projects I've been working on:

  • Computational Order and String Order
  • Quantum Computation and Correlations
  • Quantum Computation and Physical Order
  • Geodesics and Optimal Computational Schemes
  • Electron-Phonon Coupling in Quantum Computers

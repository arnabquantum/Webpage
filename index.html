About Me

I am a theoretical physics PhD student working at the intersection of quantum information and condensed matter physics. I am co-supervised by Dr. Robert Raussendorf at Leibniz University of Hannover, Germany, and Dr. Mona Berciu at the University of British Columbia, Canada.

Research

My research explores the interplay between quantum computation and condensed matter models with symmetries. I work on understanding computational phases of quantum matter and seek to uncover the fundamental sources of quantum advantage.

Projects

  • Computational Order and String Order
  • Quantum Computation and Correlations
  • Geodesics and Optimal Computational Schemes
  • Electron-Phonon Coupling in Quantum Computers
